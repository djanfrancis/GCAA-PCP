Once you purchase an air ticket to embark on a journey, have a confirmed reservation, present yourself in time for check-in or have duly checked-in, you qualify for your rights.

As a general rule, you should aim to be at the airport at least 2 hours before the scheduled departure time of your flight. Also ensure that you pay attention to the check-in time indicated on the ticket by the airline/agent

Your rights are stated in the Conditions of Carriage which forms part of the ticket you purchase. • Where you are not clear with the conditions, you may seek clarification from the airline. • You may also resort to the Ghana Civil Aviation (Economic) Directives, Part 2 - Consumer Protection, which can be found on the GCAA website - www.gcaa.com.gh

You will find displayed in the arrival, departure as well as transit halls, information in respect of the GCAA’s procedures to address passenger complaints. You will also find GCAA flyers with additional information to help you seek redress

You are entitled to benefits to be agreed between you and the air operator if you volunteer to surrender your reservation. • In the case where you were denied boarding against your will, you will be compensated at an amount to be determined by the air operator; or reimbursed; or re-routed and provided with hotel accommodation and transportation to and from the hotel or your place of accommodation where a stay of one or more nights becomes necessary as a result. • You will be offered two phone calls free of charge (at a duration to be determined), an SMS, a fax message, or an e-mail as you may prefer.

You will be reimbursed within seven days the full cost of the ticket at the price it was bought for the part or parts of the journey not made, or for the parts already made if the flight is no longer serving any purpose in relation to your original travel plan. • You will be re-routed under comparable transport conditions at the earliest opportunity or at later date at your convenience. • You will be offered meals/refreshment relative to waiting time and hotel accommodation where the stay of one or more nights becomes necessary. • The Air Operator shall not be obliged to pay compensation if it can be proven the cancellation was caused by extraordinary circumstances which could not have been avoided. • You are entitled to compensation unless you are: informed of the cancellation at least two weeks before the scheduled time of departure; or you are informed of the cancellation between two weeks to seven/three days for International/domestic flights before the scheduled time of departure and offered re-routing allowing you to depart no more than one/two hours before the scheduled time of departure and to reach your final destination less than two/one and half hours after the scheduled time of arrival respectively.

You are entitled to benefits to be agreed between you and the air operator if you volunteer to surrender your reservation. • In the case where you were denied boarding against your will, you will be compensated at an amount to be determined by the air operator; or reimbursed; or re-routed and provided with hotel accommodation and transportation to and from the hotel or your place of accommodation where a stay of one or more nights becomes necessary as a result. • You will be offered two phone calls free of charge (at a duration to be determined), an SMS, a fax message, or an e-mail as you may prefer.

If you were delayed on a Domestic Flight, you shall be offered water immediately after one hour; refreshed relative to the waiting time; and offered two (2) telephone calls, an SMS, or an e-mail as you may prefer. • If the airport is closed, you will be offered hotel accommodation and transportation to and from the hotel or your place of accommodation; or you may be reimbursed or re-routed as you may prefer. • Alternatively, if you were delayed on an International Flight, you will be offered water immediately after one (1) hour; refreshments, meals, two phone calls, SMS or e-mails between two (2) to six (6) hours after the scheduled time of departure. • Hotel accommodation, meals and transport will be provided if the expected time of departure is at least 6 hours after original time of departure. • In the event of Tarmac delay, you will be provided assistance by the Air Operator - water/refreshments/meal relative to the duration of the delay. You will also be disembarked by the Air operator when the delay exceeds three hours.
